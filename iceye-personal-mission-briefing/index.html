Post-event personalization process

From event attendee to Personal Mission Briefing.

A simple add-on for ICEYE: after the launch, each valuable attendee completes a short onboarding. ICEYE then sends a role-specific visual summary that helps them carry the SCP argument into their own organization.

Why this matters

The event is small by design. Every attendee is valuable.

Instead of sending the same recording to everyone, ICEYE can turn each attendee’s role and interest into a tailored visual briefing: sovereignty for economic buyers, architecture for technical buyers, mission-day workflow for user buyers.

The process

Five simple steps after the launch.

The flow is intentionally light. It should feel like a useful briefing service, not like a marketing survey.

1

Attendee joins the launch

They watch the shared SCP story and the buyer-specific room that matches their role.

Input: event behavior
2

Short onboarding

After the event, they answer a few simple questions: role, country context, interest area and next concern.

Input: 2 minutes
3

Role and intent mapping

ICEYE maps the attendee to the right buyer lens and identifies which argument will help them most.

Logic: segment + interest
4

Visual briefing generated

The attendee receives a concise visual summary: what SCP means for their role, what to share internally, and what to ask next.

Output: briefing card
5

Sales follow-up becomes smarter

ICEYE sales sees the attendee’s interest signals and can continue with the most relevant next conversation.

Outcome: better meeting
Three briefing versions

Same event, different takeaway.

The content is hyperpersonalized in a controlled way: by role, approved message modules and clear visual templates.

🏛️

Economic buyer brief

Focus: sovereignty, authority preserved, European capability and why federation matters now.

🧭

Technical buyer brief

Focus: architecture, tasking, permissions, prioritisation, integration and control.

🖥️

User buyer brief

Focus: mission-day workflow, requests, retasking, visibility and interface logic.

The promise is simple.

Every attendee leaves with a version of the SCP story they can actually use in their own decision context.
