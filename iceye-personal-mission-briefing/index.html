The promise

Every engaged attendee receives a brief that helps them take the next internal step.

Why this is valuable

  • Attendees get a practical internal briefing, not another event recording.
  • ICEYE sees which question matters most for the next discussion.
  • The follow-up feels precise, serious and useful.
  • The launch produces reusable proof for future conversations.

Example outputs

What the attendee receives.

Each example shows what the questionnaire plus ICEYE source material produces for the attendee: a focused brief they can use internally and a clear next step.

Example 1

Sovereignty

Attendee question: how can shared capacity support cooperation without losing authority?

Brief contains: ICEYE-approved governance logic, access principles and the internal question to resolve next.

Value: the attendee can take a clear sovereignty argument back to their organization.

Example 2

Architecture

Attendee question: how would tasking, allocation, permissions and control work in practice?

Brief contains: ICEYE-approved allocation model, control points and the recommended architecture discussion.

Value: the next meeting can start from a concrete operating model, not a general product recap.

Example 3

Workflow

Attendee question: how does an urgent observation request become tasking, confirmation and action?

Brief contains: ICEYE-approved scenario flow, key decision points and a practical workflow walkthrough.

Value: the attendee can test SCP against a real mission-day question.

Enriched insight examples

What ICEYE receives in return.

For ICEYE, questionnaire answers are combined with ICEYE’s existing data, documents and relevant public context: what the attendee needs to resolve and what the next meeting should prove.

Attendee needWhat they selected, asked or showed interest in.
Source materialICEYE data, documents, approved proof points and relevant public signals.
Next proofThe specific asset, scenario or question ICEYE should bring to the next discussion.
Enriched example 1

Maritime visibility

Profile cue: asked how teams validate maritime activity when other signals drop out.

Enrich with: recent public enforcement priority, maritime exercises and surveillance modernization.

Bring next: a scenario showing where SAR confirms what AIS, patrol aircraft or coastal radar cannot resolve.

Enriched example 2

Remote resilience

Profile cue: asked where all-weather confirmation matters for remote infrastructure.

Enrich with: Arctic strategy, infrastructure policy, disruption examples and a relevant preparedness exercise.

Bring next: a readiness map showing where all-weather SAR adds confirmation when normal monitoring fails.

Enriched example 3

Rapid tasking

Profile cue: asked how urgent observation requests move during a fast-changing situation.

Enrich with: public command structure, emergency authority, recent incident pattern and tasking capability signals.

Bring next: a request-to-decision walkthrough showing who requests, who decides and how fast the answer moves.
