Coastal surveillance and dark-vessel pressure

For a maritime country where sovereignty, illegal activity and grey-zone monitoring are becoming harder to manage with coastal systems alone.

Strategic pressureMaritime domain awareness
Capability questionPersistent all-weather visibility
Likely ownerDefence, coast guard, interior
Suggested next stepUse-case validation session

New insight hypothesis

The real opportunity may not be “more imagery”. It may be a national operating model for persistent maritime ISR where SAR confirms activity that AIS, patrol aircraft and coastal radar cannot reliably resolve.

What ICEYE should understand

  • Which maritime zones create the highest political or operational pressure.
  • Whether the need is sovereign tasking, assured access or a federated partner model.
  • How current coastal, air and naval assets fail during weather, night or surge events.

Evidence to gather

  • Recent maritime incidents, illegal fishing, sanctions evasion or dark-vessel reports.
  • National maritime strategy, coast guard modernization and defence procurement signals.
  • Participation in allied maritime exercises or regional surveillance initiatives.

Meeting angle

“Where does your current maritime picture fail when visibility, time and authority matter at the same time?”
