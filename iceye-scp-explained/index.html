The brief in 5 simple ideas

What is this launch really about?

Not a normal software announcement. The task is to make a new category easy to understand before someone else defines it.

1

ICEYE helps countries see what is happening.

Its radar satellites can observe Earth even when it is dark, cloudy, smoky or hard to see with normal cameras.

2

Speed is the point.

The value is not only getting a satellite image. The value is turning a request into useful intelligence fast enough to support a decision.

3

SCP is not the satellite.

SCP sits above mission software. It helps turn many satellites and requests into one managed operational asset.

4

Federation without losing control.

Countries can coordinate and share capacity when it helps, while keeping their own national authority and operational control intact.

5

The launch must create a mental model.

People should leave thinking: now I understand how distributed sovereign capacity can be commanded, allocated and trusted.

The three real target groups

Same launch. Three different “what does this mean for me?” answers.

The event should not speak to everyone with the same proof. Each buyer layer needs a different simple explanation.

🏛️
Economic buyers

Political leadership and EU institutions

“Shared satellite capacity does not take sovereignty away. It can help countries see more, act together, and still keep national authority.”

They need: a credible strategic voice explaining why federated sovereign capacity protects authority instead of diluting it.

🧭
Technical buyers

Military decision-makers and space command leadership

“SCP shows how satellite use is prioritised across missions, users and domains while the command chain stays clear.”

They need: a clean mental model of allocation, permissions, integration and operational control.

🖥️
User buyers

Space operations centre staff and ISR officers

“This is how the work changes on mission day: who requests, what gets priority, what the interface shows, and how faster decisions happen.”

They need: practical decision logic and enough interface clarity to advocate internally.

Recommended visual show logic

One shared broadcast, three tailored rooms.

A simple event architecture makes the audience strategy visible: everyone hears the same category claim, then receives the proof their role needs.

1. Shared openingFrame SCP as the command and allocation layer for sovereign ISR.
2. Simple visual modelShow satellites, users, priorities and authority as one understandable system.
3. Buyer splitEconomic, technical and user buyers move into role-specific content tracks.
4. Tailored proofEach room answers its own question: sovereignty, architecture or mission-day use.
5. Targeted follow-upShort clips, audio, written briefings and sales assets continue the launch after the event.
The simplest takeaway

Do not sell it as a webinar. Launch it as a new operating category.

The visual story should make SCP feel obvious: when many sovereign satellite systems need to work together, someone needs the operating layer that allocates capacity without eroding authority.
