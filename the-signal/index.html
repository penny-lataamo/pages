Plain English

What is THE SIGNAL?

A fully produced holiday celebration where guests answer a few simple questions before and during the evening. Their words and memories are turned into stage visuals, conversation moments, recognition content and a personal take-home summary after the event.

A spectacular party with a clear human idea behind it.

The theme is simple: the company’s year becomes a live broadcast. Guests do not need to understand a system or learn an app. They answer approachable prompts such as “What moment from this year deserves a replay?”, “Who helped you move forward?” and “What energy should we take into next year?”

Those answers shape the room: wall projections, host moments, table conversations, recognition highlights and the final shared “Year Signal” shown on stage.

The organisation gets a premium celebration. The guests get a night that feels personally connected to their own year.

What guests actually get

Concrete moments. Clear value after the event.

Every named element has a simple guest-facing purpose. The technology stays behind the curtain; what people see is a more personal, memorable and shareable party.

01

Personal Signal Card

A designed digital or printed card sent after the event. It includes the guest’s chosen profile type such as connector, builder or spark; 2–3 personal highlights from their answers; the company’s shared Year Signal visual; photo/video links; and one short “carry this into next year” line.

02

Year Replay Moments

During the evening, selected memories from the pre-event questions appear as beautiful anonymised or approved visuals: “moments we are proud of”, “people who helped us”, “things we want to replay”.

03

Table Conversation Prompts

Guests receive simple prompts on screens or cards, so the concept creates real conversation without forcing a workshop: “What was your best behind-the-scenes win?” “Who should get a silent standing ovation?”

04

Post-event Memory Pack

The company receives an aftermovie, selected photos, the final Year Signal visual and a short insight summary that can be reused in internal communication the following week.

Delivery scope

What is included — and what can be added.

THE SIGNAL can be delivered in three clear ways: concept-only, co-production with the client’s existing partners, or full production. The walkthrough defines the exact scope, budget logic and decision points before any commitment.

01

Concept-only

Lataamo delivers the creative concept, guest question flow, visual direction, show structure, content rules and post-event communication idea. The client or existing agencies handle production.

02

Co-production

Lataamo leads creative direction, content moderation, show dramaturgy and key experience elements while the client’s venue, catering, AV or event partners deliver agreed parts.

03

Full production

Lataamo can coordinate venue, catering, host, headliner, DJ, audiovisual production, photography, video, print, staffing and production management through agreed suppliers.

04

Cost drivers

The largest budget variables are venue, catering level, AV complexity, artist/headliner, filming scope, printed materials and staffing. These are separated early so finance can see what is core and what is optional.

Buyer essentials

What HR, finance and safety need to know.

The concept is designed to feel like a party, not a survey or workshop. The practical guardrails are agreed before production so the experience stays inclusive, safe and budget-controlled.

01

Client workload and decisions

Typical client inputs: company themes, language needs, approval rules, guest list logic, budget frame, venue constraints and final content approvals. Lataamo can provide the question set, moderation model and production schedule.

02

Inclusion and accessibility

Participation is optional, low-effort and designed to work for guests who do not want to answer, perform or use a QR code. Language versions, quiet areas, dietary needs, accessibility and sensory comfort are planned with the client.

03

Safety and event conduct

Alcohol policy, security, first aid, harassment response, crowd flow, transport and venue safety responsibilities are defined in the production plan. Sensitive or reputationally risky guest content is not shown publicly.

04

References and proof

Lataamo can share relevant event references, visual examples and sample deliverables in the walkthrough, including what a Signal Card, Year Signal visual and post-event memory pack could look like.

Procurement quick view

The commercial and risk questions up front.

This page is a concept introduction, not a fixed-price quotation. For a 500-person event, the first meeting should produce a scoped route, budget frame, responsibility split and risk checklist that finance, HR and procurement can review.

01

Included in every route

Concept adaptation, guest question logic, content moderation rules, show/story structure, experience design, approval checkpoints and a post-event communication plan.

02

Optional line items

Venue sourcing, catering, AV production, host, artist/headliner, DJ, photography, aftermovie, printed cards, extra stations, transport, security, cloakroom and first aid are scoped as separate budget lines.

Recommended timeline

Ideal start: 10–12 weeks before the event. Minimum route: around 8 weeks if venue and core suppliers are already secured. Guest questions, content approval, AV plan and safety plan each get their own lock date.

Documents for approval

The walkthrough can lead into a written scope, estimate, responsibility matrix, production timeline, privacy/DPA notes, content approval model, safety/conduct plan and sample deliverables.

Privacy and approvals

Designed to be safe for corporate use.

Participation is voluntary. Guest inputs can be anonymous or name-approved. Before launch, the client and Lataamo agree what data is collected, where it is handled, who approves public use, how long it is retained and how deletion requests are handled. AI-based elements can be fully excluded.

01

Employee consent language

The invitation can include plain-language wording on why questions are asked, how answers may be used, how anonymous/named use works and how to opt out without losing the event experience.

02

Data roles and tools

Before launch, the client receives the proposed data-role setup, tools/subprocessors, retention period and deletion process. A DPA can be prepared when required by the client’s procurement model.

03

Moderation and approvals

Only approved or anonymised content is shown. Lataamo filters sensitive material and the client confirms final public-use rules, named recognitions and edge cases before the show.

04

Image and video use

Photography, video and post-event links follow the client’s consent and communication rules, including where materials can be shared and who can access them after the event.
